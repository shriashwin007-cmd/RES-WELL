Scholarly Focus

Research Interests

Four interlocking areas of scholarly inquiry that run across my academic writing.

📖
Victorian & Gothic Literature

Sensation fiction, domestic ideology, the uncanny, and the ideological work of the realist novel in producing and contesting Victorian social norms.

  • The Gaze Reversed: Collins & Braddon
  • Architecture as Interior in Gothic Fiction
  • The Poisoned Home: Lady Audley
  • Hardy's Pastoral Ecocriticism
🌍
Postcolonial Theory & World Literature

Language as colonial wound, the politics of literary form, subaltern voice, and how African and South Asian writers negotiate inherited literary traditions.

  • Linguistic Alienation: Achebe vs Ngugi
  • Whose English? Postcolonial Language
  • Reading Rushdie Against Empire
✊
Feminist & Queer Literary Theory

Female interiority, the gaze, the body as political text, queer reading strategies, and ways literary form can be an agent of ideological resistance.

  • Embodied Dissent: Angela Carter
  • The Madwoman Reconsidered
  • Queering the Canon: Symposium Paper
  • Ernaux: Shame as Social Testimony
✍️
Modernism & Contemporary Fiction

Autofiction, narrative form as political act, consciousness and omission in modernist prose, and the contemporary novel's negotiation of testimony and memory.

  • Hemingway's Iceberg Theory
  • Autofiction & the Unstable 'I'
  • Grief as Form: Rachel Cusk

About Res Well

"I became a literary scholar because I believe stories are never just stories — they are the ideological architecture of how we understand gender, power, race, and selfhood."

I'm a final-year BA English Literature student with a specialism in Victorian literature, feminist literary theory, and postcolonial studies. My undergraduate work has earned distinctions across departments, and my dissertation on Angela Carter and feminist Gothic received the highest mark in my cohort.

I write essays that are rigorous without being impenetrable, and I believe deeply that academic criticism should speak to people beyond the seminar room. I'm currently seeking postgraduate study opportunities and professional writing work in publishing, editorial, and cultural journalism.

When I'm not in the library, I'm reading contemporary fiction, attending literary events, and contributing reviews to student publications.
